The 15 RevOps KPIs Every
Revenue Team Must Track

April 2025  ·  10 min read  ·  By Revo-Sys
Quick Answer
The five RevOps KPIs that matter most are: pipeline velocity (speed of revenue flow), MQL-to-SQL conversion rate (marketing/sales alignment), win rate (close efficiency), net revenue retention (expansion minus churn), and CAC payback period (acquisition efficiency). Every other metric is downstream of these five.

Most RevOps dashboards are bloated with vanity metrics — activity counts, email opens, login rates. These tell you what happened but not why or what to do next.

The 15 metrics below are the ones that reveal leverage: where revenue is being created, where it's leaking, and which team owns the fix. Organised by function — pipeline, conversion, retention, and efficiency.

Category 1 — Pipeline Health

These metrics tell you whether your pipeline is large enough, moving fast enough, and composed of the right deals to hit your number.

Pipeline Metrics
01 · Pipeline Velocity
Daily $ Flow
(Opps × Win Rate × ACV) ÷ Cycle Length
How much revenue your pipeline generates per day. The master metric for pipeline health — declining velocity catches problems before they hit revenue.
If declining: check # of opps, win rate, and deal size — one of the three is slipping.
02 · Pipeline Coverage Ratio
3–4× quota
Active Pipeline Value ÷ Revenue Target
How many dollars of pipeline you have per dollar of quota. The classic "pipeline coverage" metric. Less than 3× is a red flag for hitting the number.
Below 2.5×: marketing or sourcing is under-delivering pipeline in time.
03 · Average Sales Cycle
Varies by segment
Avg days from opportunity created → closed-won
Enterprise B2B: 90–180 days. SMB: 14–45 days. Rising cycle time is a leading indicator of pipeline decay — deals aging in late stages signal stall.
Benchmark against previous quarter. 10%+ increase warrants investigation.
04 · Deal Slip Rate
<15% healthy
Deals moved to next quarter ÷ Total forecasted deals
The % of pipeline that was expected to close this quarter but slipped. Above 20% indicates forecast inaccuracy, qualification issues, or late-stage objections that need a playbook.
High slip rate + low cycle time = deals aren't properly qualified at entry.

Category 2 — Conversion & Funnel Efficiency

These metrics measure how effectively your go-to-market motion converts attention into revenue at each stage.

Conversion Metrics
05 · MQL → SQL Conversion Rate
13–25% B2B
SQLs created ÷ MQLs handed off × 100
The health metric for the marketing-to-sales handoff. Below 10%: ICP mismatch or scoring model is broken. Above 35%: marketing is too conservative and under-generating.
If low: audit lead scoring criteria and ICP alignment with sales.
06 · SQL → Opportunity Rate
40–60%
Opportunities created ÷ SQLs accepted × 100
The conversion from sales-accepted lead to a live deal. Low rates indicate discovery call quality, rep skills, or qualification criteria that aren't matching reality.
Track by rep. Low performer gaps reveal coaching opportunities.
07 · Win Rate
20–30% SaaS
Closed-Won Deals ÷ Total Closed Deals × 100
The percentage of opportunities you close. Track overall AND by: rep, segment, channel, and competitive scenario. Benchmarks vary widely by deal size.
Below 15%: qualification problem. Below 10%: positioning or competitive gap.
08 · Lead Response Time
<5 minutes
Time from form submit/inbound signal → first rep contact
Responding within 5 minutes is 100× more likely to result in a qualified conversation than responding after 30 minutes. Automate with routing + instant notifications.
Even 5 min → 30 min drops qualification likelihood by 21×.

Category 3 — Revenue Retention & Expansion

For SaaS and recurring revenue businesses, what happens after the sale matters as much as acquisition. These metrics are non-negotiable for sustainable growth.

Retention Metrics
09 · Net Revenue Retention (NRR)
100%+ healthy, 120%+ elite
(Starting MRR + Expansion − Churn − Contraction) ÷ Starting MRR
The single most powerful indicator of company health. NRR above 100% means you grow revenue even without adding new customers. Best-in-class SaaS sits at 120–140%.
NRR below 90%: churn is outpacing expansion. Existential risk at scale.
10 · Gross Revenue Retention (GRR)
85–95% B2B
(Starting MRR − Churn − Contraction) ÷ Starting MRR
GRR strips out expansion and shows raw churn. High NRR but low GRR means expansion is masking a churn problem — eventually a risk as expansion headroom shrinks.
GRR below 80%: fundamental product-market fit or CS delivery issue.
11 · Customer Health Score
Composite score
Usage depth + NPS trend + support ticket frequency + expansion signals
A leading indicator of churn or expansion. Build a composite score in your CRM. Accounts dropping below threshold trigger automatic CS outreach or intervention.
Predictive: health score drops typically precede churn by 60–90 days.
12 · Time to Value (TTV)
Varies by product
Days from contract sign → first meaningful outcome for customer
The fastest path to NRR 120%+ is reducing TTV. Customers who hit their "aha moment" fast expand faster, churn less, and refer more. Measure it. Optimize it relentlessly.
TTV above 90 days for SMB is a churn predictor. Redesign onboarding.

Category 4 — Efficiency & GTM Economics

These are the board-level metrics that determine whether your growth is capital-efficient and sustainable.

Efficiency Metrics
13 · CAC Payback Period
12–18 months SaaS
CAC ÷ (ACV × Gross Margin)
How many months it takes to recover the cost of acquiring a customer. Under 12 months is excellent. Over 24 months raises capital efficiency concerns at scale.
Above 24 months: either CAC is too high or ACV/margin needs improvement.
14 · Revenue per FTE
$150–300K/FTE early stage
Total Revenue ÷ Total Full-Time Employees
Measures operational leverage. Rising revenue per FTE means you're scaling efficiently. Declining means headcount is outpacing revenue growth — the #1 sign of over-hiring into GTM.
Best-in-class SaaS at IPO: $300K–600K+ per FTE.
15 · Magic Number
>0.75 efficient
(ARR growth over quarter × 4) ÷ Prior quarter S&M spend
SaaS efficiency metric showing how much ARR you generate per dollar of sales & marketing spend. Above 0.75 = good. Above 1.0 = excellent. Below 0.5 = rethink GTM motion.
Below 0.5: GTM spend is not generating proportionate revenue growth.
Bonus · Ramp Time to Quota
3–6 months avg
Days from hire → first month at 100% quota attainment
How fast new reps contribute. Longer ramp = higher carrying cost + talent risk. Cut ramp time with better onboarding, strong playbooks, and CRM data hygiene so reps inherit clean accounts.
Best: ramp <90 days. Above 180 days: onboarding or territory quality issue.

Building Your RevOps Dashboard

The right dashboard puts these 15 metrics in front of the right people at the right cadence:

  • Weekly (operational): Pipeline velocity, lead response time, deal slip rate, MQL→SQL rate
  • Monthly (revenue): Win rate, ACV, sales cycle, NRR, GRR, CAC payback
  • Quarterly (strategic): Magic number, revenue per FTE, ramp time, health score trends

Never mix cadences in one dashboard. A CEO doesn't need daily deal activity. A SDR manager doesn't need quarterly magic number. Segment by audience and update automatically.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important RevOps KPIs?
The five that matter most: pipeline velocity, MQL-to-SQL conversion rate, win rate, net revenue retention (NRR), and CAC payback period. Every other metric is downstream of these five. Master these before expanding your dashboard.
What is a good MQL-to-SQL conversion rate?
A good B2B MQL-to-SQL conversion rate is 13–25%. Below 10% usually indicates ICP misalignment between marketing and sales, or a broken lead scoring model. Above 35% may mean marketing is being too conservative and under-generating pipeline.
What is pipeline velocity and how do you calculate it?
Pipeline velocity = (# of opportunities × Win Rate × Average Deal Size) ÷ Sales Cycle Length in days. It measures how much revenue your pipeline generates per day. Track it weekly — a decline in velocity is a leading indicator of a revenue shortfall 30–60 days out.
What is a good win rate for B2B SaaS?
A good win rate for B2B SaaS is 20–30% of qualified opportunities. Enterprise typically runs 15–20%; SMB can reach 30–40%. Win rates below 15% usually signal either poor qualification (bad-fit deals in the pipeline) or competitive positioning gaps.
