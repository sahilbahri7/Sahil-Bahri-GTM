Lead Scoring: The Complete B2B Guide
to Prioritising Your Best Leads

REVO-SYS · APRIL 2025 · 8 MIN READ
Quick Answer

Lead scoring assigns a numerical score to each prospect based on their demographic fit (job title, company size, industry) and behavioural engagement (page views, email opens, form fills). High-scoring leads get prioritised for sales outreach; low-scoring leads stay in nurture flows.

The Two Dimensions of Lead Scoring

Dimension 1: Fit Score (Demographic)

The fit score measures how well a lead matches your Ideal Customer Profile. This is profile-based — about who they are, not what they do.

Dimension 2: Engagement Score (Behavioural)

The engagement score measures how interested a lead appears based on their actions. High engagement signals buying intent.

The Lead Scoring Matrix

Use both dimensions together to make routing decisions:

Hot Lead — Route to Sales Now
High fit + High engagement. Assign and follow up within 24 hours.
Nurture — Stay in Marketing
Low fit + High engagement. Continue marketing, monitor closely.
Qualify — Targeted Outbound
High fit + Low engagement. Proactive, personalised outbound touch.
Pass — Low Touch
Low fit + Low engagement. Low-touch nurture or discard.

Score Decay: Keeping Your Model Fresh

Lead scores should decay over time. A lead who visited your pricing page 6 months ago and has done nothing since is not the same as one who did it yesterday. Build score decay rules: reduce behavioural scores by 50% every 30 days of inactivity, and reset to zero after 90 days of no engagement.

AI-Powered Lead Scoring

Modern CRMs (HubSpot Enterprise, Salesforce Einstein) offer AI-powered predictive scoring that trains machine learning models on your actual conversion data. Instead of manually assigning point values, the AI identifies which attribute combinations statistically predict conversion — and weights them accordingly. AI scoring typically outperforms manual scoring by 15–25% after 3–6 months of training data.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is lead scoring?
Lead scoring is a methodology for ranking prospects using a numerical score based on demographic fit (job title, company size, industry) and behavioural engagement (page views, email opens, demo requests).
How do you build a lead scoring model?
Analyse your closed-won deals for common attributes, assign point values to positive and negative signals, set an MQL threshold, implement in your CRM, then calibrate based on actual conversion data over 90 days.
What is the difference between lead scoring and lead grading?
Lead scoring measures behavioural engagement. Lead grading measures demographic fit. Best programs use both dimensions to route leads appropriately.
How often should you update your lead scoring model?
Review your model quarterly. Compare the scores of leads who converted vs. those who didn't and adjust weights accordingly.
