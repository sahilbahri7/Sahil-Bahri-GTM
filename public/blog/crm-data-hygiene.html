CRM Data Hygiene:
How to Clean & Maintain Your CRM

REVO-SYS · APRIL 2025 · 8 MIN READ
Quick Answer

CRM data hygiene is the practice of keeping your CRM records accurate, complete, and up-to-date. Poor hygiene leads to wasted sales effort, broken automations, and untrustworthy reports. The fix is a one-time cleanup plus automated prevention processes that stop it from getting dirty again.

Why CRM Data Goes Bad

CRM data degrades at approximately 30% per year. Contacts change jobs, companies get acquired, email addresses bounce, and phone numbers go stale. Meanwhile, reps enter data inconsistently, integrations create duplicate records, and no one has time to clean it up. The result: your sales team burns time on bad numbers, email campaigns hit 40% bounce rates, and your revenue forecast is based on phantom deals.

The 6 Most Common CRM Data Problems

Duplicate Records
Same contact created twice via different sources. Reps call the same person from two records.
Missing Fields
Company size, industry, or owner missing. Lead scoring and routing break silently.
Inconsistent Values
"United States", "US", "USA" as separate values. Segmentation becomes unreliable.
Stale Records
Contacts who left their company 2 years ago still active in your pipeline.
Zombie Deals
Deals not updated in 90+ days inflating your pipeline and misleading forecasts.
Personal Emails
Gmail/Yahoo contacts with no company association — not real B2B leads.

The CRM Data Audit Process

Step 1: Pull a data health report

In HubSpot: use the Data Quality Command Center or build a list of contacts with missing key properties. In Salesforce: use Data Quality Analysis Dashboards. Key fields to check: company name, email validity, job title, lifecycle stage, last activity date, owner.

Step 2: Deduplicate

Run your CRM's built-in dedup tool. Merge strategy: always keep the record with more complete data and more recent activity as the primary. Never delete the secondary record immediately — archive it first.

Step 3: Enrich missing data

Use data enrichment tools to automatically fill in missing company size, industry, revenue, and contact details. Options: HubSpot Data Enrichment (built-in), Clearbit, ZoomInfo, Apollo, or Clay for custom enrichment workflows.

Step 4: Standardise field values

Build picklists for fields like Country, Industry, and Company Size instead of letting reps type free-form values. Retroactively update inconsistent values using bulk edit or a workflow that maps variations to a standard value.

Step 5: Archive stale records

For contacts with no activity in 24+ months and no open opportunities, move them to an "Archived" lifecycle stage and suppress from all active marketing. Don't delete — you may need them for compliance or historical reporting.

Step 6: Set up prevention workflows

Build workflows that alert the CRM owner when duplicates are detected, flag records with missing required fields, trigger enrichment on every new contact creation, and automatically close stale deals after 90 days of no activity.

Quarterly CRM Data Hygiene Checklist
Run deduplication sweep — merge all flagged duplicates
Review contacts with missing Company, Owner, or Lifecycle Stage
Enrich stale records (no activity in 60+ days)
Audit email bounce rates — remove or re-validate bouncing addresses
Close or archive deals with no activity in 90 days
Review and update pipeline stage definitions with sales team
Check integration logs for sync errors creating bad records
Update lead routing rules if team or territory structure has changed

Frequently Asked Questions

What is CRM data hygiene?
CRM data hygiene is the ongoing practice of ensuring your CRM records are accurate, complete, deduplicated, and up-to-date. It includes removing duplicates, standardising field values, filling missing data, and archiving outdated records.
How do you clean CRM data?
Run a deduplication sweep, identify and fill missing critical fields via enrichment, standardise inconsistent values with picklists, and archive records with no activity in 2+ years. Then set up automated prevention workflows to stop data from getting dirty again.
How often should you clean your CRM?
Run a full audit quarterly. Automated hygiene workflows should run continuously. CRM data degrades at ~30% per year as contacts change jobs and companies evolve.
Why does bad CRM data hurt revenue?
Bad data causes: wasted sales time calling wrong numbers, broken automation workflows, inaccurate lead scoring, misleading forecasts, and failed email campaigns. Studies show sales reps lose 30+ minutes per day to bad CRM data.
