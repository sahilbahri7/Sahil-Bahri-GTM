What Is a Sales Funnel?
B2B Stages, Metrics & Optimization

By Sahil Bahri  ·  April 10, 2025  ·  8 min read

QUICK ANSWER

A sales funnel is the journey a prospect takes from first awareness of your product to becoming a paying customer. It's called a funnel because many people enter at the top but fewer convert at each stage. The goal of managing a sales funnel is to maximize conversion at every step.

The B2B Sales Funnel Stages

TOFU

Awareness — Top of Funnel

Prospect first learns your company exists. Sources: SEO, social media, paid ads, events, word of mouth. Goal: drive traffic and generate leads. Content: blog posts, LinkedIn content, webinars, ads.

MOFU

Interest & Education — Middle of Funnel

Prospect is researching solutions. They've identified a problem and are evaluating options. Goal: position your solution as the best fit. Content: case studies, comparison guides, demo videos, webinars, email nurture.

BOFU

Consideration & Evaluation — Bottom of Funnel

Prospect is actively evaluating you vs. competitors. They've likely had a demo, talked to references, and are analyzing pricing. Goal: remove objections and build confidence. Activities: proof of concept, ROI analysis, legal/security review, references.

CLOSE

Decision & Purchase

Prospect becomes a customer. Contract negotiation, final approvals, procurement. Goal: close with favorable terms and set up a strong onboarding handoff. Activities: negotiation, contract execution, kickoff call scheduling.

POST

Retention & Expansion

Customer is onboarded and using the product. Goal: drive adoption, reduce churn, and create expansion revenue. Activities: onboarding, QBRs, health scoring, upsell/cross-sell motions, referral programs.

How to Optimize Each Funnel Stage

Top of Funnel (TOFU) Optimization

  • Focus on high-intent SEO keywords (buyers searching for solutions)
  • Create lead magnets aligned to your ICP's biggest problems
  • Use LinkedIn thought leadership to build trust before outbound
  • Run retargeting ads to warm website visitors

Middle of Funnel (MOFU) Optimization

  • Build automated nurture sequences (5–7 email series) triggered by content downloads
  • Create comparison content ("Us vs. Competitor") for buyers doing research
  • Use lead scoring to identify which MOFU leads are ready for sales outreach
  • Host monthly webinars or virtual demos for engaged prospects

Bottom of Funnel (BOFU) Optimization

  • Create a structured evaluation process — don't let deals meander without next steps
  • Build an ROI calculator that quantifies the value of your solution
  • Develop a reference customer program so prospects can speak to similar companies
  • Identify and engage all stakeholders in the buying committee (not just your champion)

Post-Sale Optimization

  • Define a standardized onboarding journey with clear milestones
  • Build customer health scores to identify at-risk accounts early
  • Create a quarterly business review (QBR) framework
  • Design expansion triggers — identify when customers are ready for upsell

Sales Funnel vs. Flywheel

The traditional funnel has one weakness: it treats customer acquisition as the finish line. The modern "flywheel" model (popularized by HubSpot) recognizes that happy customers generate referrals and expansion revenue — feeding new prospects back into the top of the funnel. RevOps connects the two: funnel-based acquisition metrics at the front, retention and expansion metrics at the back.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a sales funnel?
A sales funnel represents the journey a prospect takes from first becoming aware of your product to becoming a paying customer. It's called a funnel because many prospects enter at the top but fewer convert at each stage.
What are the stages of a B2B sales funnel?
The typical B2B sales funnel stages are: Awareness (TOFU), Interest/Education (MOFU), Consideration/Evaluation (BOFU), Decision/Purchase, and Post-Sale (Retention & Expansion).
How do you measure sales funnel performance?
Measure with stage-by-stage conversion rates, average time in each stage, pipeline velocity, and lead-to-close rates. The most important metric is stage conversion rate — the percentage advancing from one stage to the next.
What's the difference between a sales funnel and a pipeline?
A pipeline represents active deals that sales is working. A funnel includes the full journey — including marketing stages before a lead becomes a sales opportunity. The pipeline is the BOFU portion of the broader funnel.
