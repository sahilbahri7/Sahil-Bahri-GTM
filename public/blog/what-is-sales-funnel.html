What Is a Sales Funnel?
B2B Stages, Metrics & Optimisation

REVO-SYS · APRIL 2025 · 8 MIN READ
Quick Answer

A sales funnel is the journey a prospect takes from first awareness of your product to becoming a paying customer. It's called a funnel because many people enter at the top but fewer convert at each stage. The goal: maximise conversion at every step.

The B2B Sales Funnel Stages

TOFU

Awareness — Top of Funnel

Prospect first learns your company exists. Sources: SEO, social media, paid ads, events, word of mouth. Content: blog posts, LinkedIn, webinars, ads.

MOFU

Interest & Education — Middle of Funnel

Prospect is researching solutions. Content: case studies, comparison guides, demo videos, email nurture sequences.

BOFU

Consideration & Evaluation — Bottom of Funnel

Prospect is actively evaluating you vs. competitors. Activities: proof of concept, ROI analysis, legal/security review, references.

CLOSE

Decision & Purchase

Contract negotiation, final approvals, procurement. Activities: negotiation, contract execution, kickoff call scheduling.

POST

Retention & Expansion

Goal: drive adoption, reduce churn, and create expansion revenue. Activities: onboarding, QBRs, health scoring, upsell/cross-sell motions.

How to Optimise Each Funnel Stage

Top of Funnel (TOFU)

  • Focus on high-intent SEO keywords (buyers searching for solutions)
  • Create lead magnets aligned to your ICP's biggest problems
  • Use LinkedIn thought leadership to build trust before outbound

Middle of Funnel (MOFU)

  • Build automated nurture sequences triggered by content downloads
  • Create comparison content for buyers doing vendor research
  • Use lead scoring to identify which MOFU leads are ready for sales outreach

Bottom of Funnel (BOFU)

  • Create a structured evaluation process with clear next steps
  • Build an ROI calculator that quantifies your solution's value
  • Identify and engage all stakeholders in the buying committee

Post-Sale

  • Define a standardised onboarding journey with clear milestones
  • Build customer health scores to identify at-risk accounts early
  • Design expansion triggers — when are customers ready for upsell?

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a sales funnel?
A sales funnel represents the journey a prospect takes from first becoming aware of your product to becoming a paying customer. It's called a funnel because many prospects enter at the top but fewer convert at each stage.
What are the stages of a B2B sales funnel?
Awareness (TOFU), Interest/Education (MOFU), Consideration/Evaluation (BOFU), Decision/Purchase, and Post-Sale (Retention & Expansion).
How do you measure sales funnel performance?
Measure with stage-by-stage conversion rates, average time in each stage, pipeline velocity, and lead-to-close rates. The most important metric is stage conversion rate — the percentage advancing from one stage to the next.
What's the difference between a sales funnel and a pipeline?
A pipeline represents active deals that sales is working. A funnel includes the full journey — including marketing stages before a lead becomes a sales opportunity. The pipeline is the BOFU portion of the broader funnel.
