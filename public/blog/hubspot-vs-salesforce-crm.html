HubSpot vs Salesforce:
Which CRM Wins in 2025?

By Sahil Bahri  ·  April 10, 2025  ·  9 min read

QUICK ANSWER

Choose HubSpot if you're an SMB or mid-market company that wants fast setup, built-in marketing automation, and a lower total cost. Choose Salesforce if you're an enterprise with highly complex custom processes, a large Salesforce admin team, and deep integration requirements across legacy systems.

The Core Trade-off

HubSpot and Salesforce are both world-class CRMs, but they're optimized for very different buyers. HubSpot is built for speed-to-value and usability. Salesforce is built for maximum flexibility and enterprise scale. The right choice depends almost entirely on your company's size, technical resources, and operational complexity.

Pricing: The Real Story

HubSpot Pricing

  • Free CRM: Unlimited users, core contact and deal management
  • Sales Hub Starter: ~$20/user/month — removes HubSpot branding, adds email sequences
  • Sales Hub Professional: ~$90/user/month — forecasting, playbooks, custom reporting
  • Sales Hub Enterprise: ~$150/user/month — predictive lead scoring, advanced permissions, custom objects

Salesforce Pricing

  • Starter Suite: $25/user/month — basic CRM only
  • Professional: $80/user/month — most commonly used tier
  • Enterprise: $165/user/month — workflow automation, advanced customization
  • Unlimited: $330/user/month — full suite with Einstein AI
  • Hidden costs: Implementation ($20k–$200k), ongoing admin ($80-120k/yr), add-ons like Marketing Cloud, CPQ, Service Cloud

Real-World Cost Comparison

A 20-person sales team on HubSpot Pro: ~$1,800/month all-in. The same team on Salesforce Enterprise with a part-time admin and basic integrations: ~$4,500–7,000/month. Over 3 years, HubSpot typically saves $150k–300k at this company size.

When to Choose HubSpot

  • You're a startup or SMB (under 500 employees)
  • Your marketing team wants to own the CRM without heavy IT involvement
  • You need marketing automation and CRM in one platform
  • You don't have a dedicated Salesforce admin on staff
  • You want to be up and running in 30 days, not 6 months
  • Your sales process is relatively standard (not hyper-customized)

When to Choose Salesforce

  • You're an enterprise (1,000+ employees) with complex org-wide processes
  • You have dedicated Salesforce admins and developers on staff
  • You need deep integration with legacy ERP systems (SAP, Oracle)
  • Your sales process has highly custom objects, workflows, or territory hierarchies
  • Your investors or board expect Salesforce (common in later-stage B2B SaaS)
  • You need CPQ, Field Service, or advanced Financial Services capabilities

The Migration Question

If you're on Salesforce and considering a move to HubSpot, you're not alone. Many mid-market companies switch to HubSpot after realizing the Salesforce total cost of ownership is untenable without a large ops team. The migration is very doable — but requires careful data mapping, deduplication, and a transition plan that keeps both systems running in parallel for at least 60 days.

If you're on HubSpot and considering Salesforce, make sure you're doing it for the right reasons (enterprise requirements, not just prestige) — and budget at least $50k for implementation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is HubSpot better than Salesforce?
HubSpot is better for SMBs and mid-market companies that need fast time-to-value, built-in marketing tools, and lower total cost of ownership. Salesforce is better for enterprise companies with complex custom processes and dedicated Salesforce admin resources.
How much does HubSpot cost vs Salesforce?
HubSpot Sales Hub Professional runs ~$90/user/month. Salesforce Enterprise runs $165/user/month plus significant hidden costs for implementation, add-ons, and admin. Real all-in costs typically make Salesforce 2-3x more expensive for mid-market teams.
Can you migrate from Salesforce to HubSpot?
Yes. HubSpot has a dedicated migration path and certified partners. Key steps include mapping data objects, cleaning records before export, and running both systems in parallel during the 60-90 day transition.
Which CRM is easier to use?
HubSpot consistently wins on ease of use. Its UI is more intuitive, adoption rates are higher, and new reps can onboard themselves without extended training. Salesforce requires more structured training and admin support.
